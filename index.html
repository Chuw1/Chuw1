About Me

I will be joining the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Texas A&M University as an Instructional Assistant Professor in Fall 2024. My research focuses primarily on Artificial intelligence, Machine Learning, Robotics, and Computer Vision. I was born in the coastal city of Qingdao, Shan Dong, China, known for its picturesque beaches and vibrant culture. I earned my Master of Science degree from the Computer Science Department at Boston University in 2016, and my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Houston in 2021, where my work concentrated on computational methods for MRI-guided and powered ferric applicators, including modeling and image processing.

Before my appointment at Texas A&M, I served as a visiting professor at the University of Houston-Victoria.

Teaching Philosophy

I have a profound passion for teaching as it allows me to effectively impart knowledge to future generations. I take great care in planning my classes, focusing on condensing key topics that will benefit students in the future. Teaching computer science, especially programming courses, presents unique challenges, such as conveying fundamental coding logic and algorithms through limited examples. However, the fulfillment I experience when my students successfully create impressive programs is immensely rewarding.

My educational approach is founded on three fundamental principles: encouraging analytical thought, ensuring a comprehensive grasp of topics, and supporting the personal and academic development of each student. I emphasize real-world problem-solving and analytical skills, fostering an environment where students can deconstruct, analyze, and create innovative solutions.

I aim to ignite students' genuine interest in the subject matter by incorporating captivating topics and demonstrating practical applications. My teaching philosophy strongly believes in student-centered education, where students understand the relevance of their knowledge for future endeavors.
