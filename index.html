About Me

I will join the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Texas A&M University as an Instructional Assistant Professor in Fall 2024. My research primarily focuses on machine learning, computer vision, and medical image processing. I earned my Ph.D. from the University of Houston in 2021, where my work concentrated on Computational Methods for MRI-Guided and Powered Ferric Applicators: Modeling and Image Processing.

Before my appointment at Texas A&M, I served as a visiting professor at the University of Houston-Victoria.

Teaching Philosophy

I have a passion for teaching because it allows me to effectively transmit knowledge to future generations. I take great care in planning my classes and focus on condensing key topics that will be beneficial for students in the future. Teaching computer science, particularly programming-related courses, presents challenges as expressing fundamental coding logic and algorithms through a limited number of examples can be difficult. Nevertheless, the fulfillment I experience when witnessing my students successfully create impressive programs is truly rewarding.

I believe in fostering a collaborative and inclusive learning environment where students are encouraged to think critically and apply their knowledge to real-world scenarios. My teaching approach emphasizes hands-on experience, project-based learning, and the integration of current industry practices into the curriculum.

Teaching

Spring 2024 Semester

COSC 3317 - Object Oriented Programming

Undergraduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2024

A study of concepts of Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) technology including but not limited to Abstract data types, encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism. The purpose of the course is to provide understanding of the theoretical and practical foundations on object-oriented programming (OOP). The course will step from the ground up in OOP providing the students a comprehensive experience. Assignments will enable students to practice with many types of programming topics.

COSC 6328 - Deep Learning

Graduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2024

The Introduction to Deep Learning Applications and Theory course is a graduate-level program designed to equip students with essential skills, foundational concepts, and practical applications of deep learning and neural networks for analyzing complex data sets and system.

COSC 4360 - Senior Project

Undergraduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2024

In this course, students are required to deliver a software product and a final senior project report, which will go through the stages of requirements, analysis, design, implementation, testing, and evaluation. At the end of the semester, students must present their senior project report.

The course objectives are to develop students' abilities to draw reasonable inferences from observations and learn to formulate and solve new computer science problems using analytical and problem-solving skills. Additionally, students will develop the ability to synthesize and integrate information and ideas, think creatively, think holistically, and distinguish between facts and opinions.

students will cultivate the ability to work both individually and as part of a team, commit to accurate work, and develop management skills. The course will help improve their speaking and writing skills, ability to follow directions and plans, and ability to organize and use time effectively. Additionally, students will cultivate the ability to work skillfully for personal achievement, gain an informed understanding of the role of science and technology, foster a lifelong love of learning, and cultivate a sense of responsibility for their own behavior while improving self-esteem and self-confidence

COSC 6379 - Data Science in Healthcare

Graduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2024

This course aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary for graduate research in medical image computing. Students will gain an in-depth understanding of state-of-the-art approaches to various topics. By the end of the course, they will have acquired essential research skills, including writing paper reviews, presenting technical papers, analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of research papers, and identifying open questions and future research directions

COSC 6342 - Software Engineering Project Management

Graduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2024

This course introduces students to the comprehensive process of software product and project management, covering the entire life cycle of software production. This includes requirement analysis, design and modeling, development, quality assurance, testing, and deployment. Through the study of theories, applications, and case studies, students will learn to conduct effective system analysis, design, and management within large organizations. This will involve navigating complex interactions among various roles, diverse systems, and business environments

Fall 2023 Semester

COSC 6345 - Design and Analysis of Algorithms

Graduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2023

This course involves the study of algorithm design, including time and space complexity analysis, divide and conquer strategies, the greedy method, dynamic programming, search and traversal techniques, backtracking, and an examination of NP-complete and NP-hard problems.

COSC 6349 - Computer Vision and Image Processing

Graduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2023

This course introduces fundamental concepts and techniques in image processing and computer vision. Key topics include image formation, edge detection and segmentation, color theory, perspective analysis, and applications of machine learning

GMNG 4322 - Game Artificial Intelligence and Behavioral Modeling

Undergraduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2023

This course covers a range of topics in artificial intelligence, including heuristic search, minimax search, alpha-beta pruning, and strategy game playing. It also delves into semantic networks, propositional and predicate logic, modal logic, expert systems, machine learning, neural networks, artificial life, learning through emergent behavior, genetic algorithms, intelligent agents, natural language understanding, and machine vision

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to understand heuristic search and how minimax search is applied in games. They will be able to generate and implement their own AI project ideas in a game context. Additionally, students will develop the skills to work and communicate effectively in a team setting

COSC 4320 - Software Engineering

Undergraduate course, University of Houston-Victoria, Computer Science Department, 2023

Upon successful completion of this course, students will understand the distinction between functional and non-functional requirements, as well as user and system requirements, and be able to write a Software Requirements Specification document using requirements engineering processes. They will implement design methods such as object-oriented (OO) design and Unified Modeling Language (UML), and work with various software life-cycle models, including the waterfall model, V model, rapid prototyping model, incremental model, and spiral model. Students will generate and deliver a software product based on their own project idea, effectively work and communicate within a team, and comprehend the ethical considerations in software engineering.
